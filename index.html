The Cesar Cipher

The Cesar Cipher is one of the oldest encryption methods known to man. It is named after Julius Cesar, who used it to send encrypted messages to his subordinates.

This encryption method takes all the letters of a plaintext and applies a shift. The shift is a number which dictates how far down the alphabet you must jump to find the encrypted letter. For instance, if we have the letter "E" in plaintext and apply a shift of 3 we would end up with a "B".
